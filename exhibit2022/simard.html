an installation questions the complex relationship between

the ethereal nature of the human mind.

Introduction

In Paméla's work, she tries to address issues of mental illness and the body as a whole. To do so, she combines different methodologies of work and materials such as wood, textiles and photography. The installation she created for this exhibition is a diptych, including two individual artworks. One is a digitally printed portrait on silk where rows of threads are systematically unraveled. To her, the idea of unraveling one's image addresses the issue of trauma; of losing one's identity. The other component is a woodworking representation of glial cells. Both sculptures were created based on research images she obtained from Dr. Naguib Mechawar, a researcher in Montreal who investigates molecular neuroanatomy of major depression and suicide at the Douglas Institute. Altogether, this installation questions the complex relationship between the human brain as a tangible organ and the ethereal nature of the human mind.
