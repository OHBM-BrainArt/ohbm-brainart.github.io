a multimodal installation that aims to represent the complexity of language processing.

Introduction

s.cer.log is a multimodal and interactive installation that aims to represent the complexity of language processing through sounds and music.

All the different facets of language reflect the intricacy of the mechanisms that support this faculty: the ability to understand, produce and organize language for an efficient communication requires the coordinate interplay of specific cortical regions. s.cer.log emphasizes the role of white matter connections in mediating this integration and highlights how complete intelligibility of language arises only when there is coordination within a defined structural network.

The interactive nature of the project wants to engage even a non-specialized public in the understanding of these complex but fundamental mechanisms that enable language and reflect constituent principles of general cognitive functioning.

Relation to "The Connected Brain"

The artist chose to focus on integration rather than cortical segregation to explain how our brain processes complex information. s.cer.log stresses that no cortical region stands alone, and that the way in which different brain areas communicate with each other is not random, but finely organized and bounded to white matter connections.
