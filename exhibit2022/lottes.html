Erin Lottes

A series of brains become more visually disparate as they approach the edges of the piece,
changed by fMRI preprocessing and the artist's imperfect hand.

Introduction

Erin's piece is entitled Butterfly Effects. The art is inspired by Xinhui Li's recent work on fMRI preprocessing methods, in which she - in collaboration with others - found that the use of different preprocessing pipelines could result in different quantified data and therefore different findings. They thought it was interesting how one small decision early on in preprocessing could change so much, and so they decided to make a piece about the butterfly effect in the shape of a butterfly. In their piece, different preprocessed images of the same brain slice are "processed" in artistic ways - sometimes traced, sometimes painted or sponged, even embroidered. As one traces the butterfly to the edge, the images become more different from their original sources.

Relation to "The Connected Brain"

It is, quite literally, a map of connected brains!

Fun Story

They originally pinned the images on a piece of foam board and connected them by winding multicolor thread around the pins. However, that was not sturdy enough to withstand international travel, so they have made a poster version.
