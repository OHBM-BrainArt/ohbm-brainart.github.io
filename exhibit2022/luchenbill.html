Introduction

This project investigates aspects of anxiety and memory, and the effects that war trauma has on certain memories stored in the brain. The images he constructs are based upon his perceptions of what happens when traumatic memories are created and stored in the brain. In his experience, memories are consolidated, stacked and stored on top of one another which results in lost and fragmented information and formatted in a skewed sense of time. In this series, he uses images from his first deployment to Iraq that are autobiographical, complicated them through strategies of overlapping, interruption and fragmentation to reflect the sense of anxiety that is the by-product of being placed in a war zone.

Relation to "The Connected Brain"

The primary focus of his work is looking at how he interprets visually on how certain memories are stored or "connected" inside the brain, and what certain situations and certain actions affect the connectivity of these memories.
