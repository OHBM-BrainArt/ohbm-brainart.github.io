an interactive installation Ã  l'odeur representing the five emotions:
love, anxiety, happiness, sadness and aggression.

Introduction

Anne's early childhood has been strongly affected by her olfactory sense, which draws an existential role in her life. On the basis of the fact that human language contains the expression: the smell of fear (cold sweat) and due to my understanding of molecules, the question evidently occurs: What about the odour of other emotions? Several behavioural, perceptual and brain mapping studies prove her thesis and show the reaction and processing of the different chemosensory emotion signals. Humans do respond to the odour (sweat) of anxiety, love, happiness, sadness and aggression, which means we are communicating via chemosensory signals.

Relation to "The Connected Brain"

The human bulbus olfactorius does not differentiate between colour and origin, so if someone is scared our olfactory sense simply focuses on the information of the anxiety chemosensory signal and responds to it. The human sense of olfaction is a truly important sense in human communication, it connects us to each other and the world we are living in.

Fun Story

While collecting the sweat of her fellow men, Anne understood the story and behaviour of Jean-Baptiste Grenouille more and more. Sometimes it still seems as if the world is scared to smell.
